# billliang-ai

## Bill (Ping) Liang

Researcher-builder working at the intersection of **autonomous AI systems** and **real businesses**.

I study what happens when you let AI agents actually run things — not as tools, but as operators.

---

### What I'm working on

- **AI Autonomous Operations** — engineering patterns for systems where AI agents handle the full operational loop (decision → execution → iteration), with humans only in strategic positions.
- **EEG Signal Processing** — my PhD research on EEG signal processing. The same architectural ideas (specialization, routing, self-organization) translate directly to multi-agent AI systems.
- **Reservoir Computing × LLMs** — exploring whether reservoir-based components can replace or augment parts of transformer-based agents for more efficient autonomous decision-making.

### Where I come from

- PhD researcher in Electronics Engineering at **Batangas State University** (Philippines), focused on biomedical signal processing.
- Published at **SiPS 2025**, **HNICEM 2025**. Q1 SCI manuscript under review.
- Cross-cultural operations and B2B sales lead in Southern Africa (SADC region).
- Native Mandarin, fluent English, learning Setswana and Tagalog.

### Why I build in public

I don't believe in selling certainty I don't have. The frontier of "AI running businesses" is unmapped. I'd rather show my real experiments — the ones that work, the ones that fail, and the engineering patterns that emerge — than write polished tutorials about things everyone already knows.

Every commit is a piece of an answer to one question:

> **In 2026, where exactly is the line between what AI can autonomously do, and what still needs a human?**

I'm trying to find that line by crossing it, repeatedly, in small experiments.
